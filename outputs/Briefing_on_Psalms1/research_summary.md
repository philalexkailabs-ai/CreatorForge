Psalm 1, the first psalm in the Book of Psalms, is attributed to King David and structured as a poetic contrast between the righteous and the wicked, followed by a blessing for the righteous. It emphasizes living righteously, avoiding sinners, and the consequences of straying from God’s ways. The righteous are metaphorically described as a tree planted by streams of water, symbolizing stability and divine sustenance. The psalm’s historical context dates to David’s time (c. 10th–11th century BCE) and underscores God’s justice, obedience, and eternal favor.
